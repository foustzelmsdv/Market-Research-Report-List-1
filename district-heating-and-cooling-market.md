<p><p>District heating and cooling markets refer to centralized systems that provide thermal energy to multiple buildings from a single source. District heating involves the distribution of hot water or steam for space heating and domestic hot water, often utilizing renewable energy or waste heat. Conversely, district cooling delivers chilled water for air conditioning, leveraging efficient processes like absorption chilling. Both systems enhance energy efficiency, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and support sustainable urban development by optimizing resource use and minimizing environmental impact.</p></p>

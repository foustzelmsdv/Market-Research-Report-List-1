<p><p>The Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM) market is segmented into Constant-Current Mode and Constant-Height Mode. In Constant-Current Mode, the STM maintains a consistent tunneling current as the probe moves over the sample, enabling detailed topographic imaging. Conversely, Constant-Height Mode keeps the probe at a fixed height, monitoring variations in tunneling current to detect surface features. Both modes are crucial for nanoscale imaging, each offering unique advantages for specific applications in materials science and nanotechnology research.</p></p>
